YOUTH DAY CELEBRATION

What Youth Day means for South Africa:

Youth Day is a very important for all of us South Africans.
It is a day where we as South Africans honour the youths of
1976 who were ambushed by the apartheid regime police during
their peaceful protest in Soweto, where approximately 500 students
were killed.

16 June 1976

On the 16th of June 1976, thousands of black youths took to the streets of Soweto,Gauteng,which was known as the Transvaal back then to protest against against the law which had introduced Afrikaans as the medium of instruction in black township schools.

How many people showed up?:

It is said that approximately, if not more than about 10 000 students attended the protest.

Involvement of the police:

When the police were called in to disperse the crowd, the peaceful protest took a turn for the worst. When the police arrived at the scene of the protest, they arrived with dogs and weapons and started by using tear gas oninnocent students and youths. And when the youths started fighting back, bullets started flying all over the scene as a means to try and control the students as they were fighting back.
